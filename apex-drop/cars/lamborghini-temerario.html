Lamborghini Temerario

The Huracán's hybrid successor is in owners' hands, and it's the last Lamborghini for a while with this much drama in its exhaust note.

Read the analysis →

The Temerario is Lamborghini's answer to a question every mid-engine supercar maker is wrestling with right now: how do you keep the drama of a naturally aspirated V10 while meeting emissions targets that all but require turbocharging and electrification?

Lamborghini's solution is a twin-turbo V8 with hybrid assist, producing 907 hp — down on cylinder count from the outgoing Huracán's V10, but up on outright output and a full second quicker to 62 mph.

Deliveries are already underway, and early owner feedback has focused less on the numbers and more on how convincingly Lamborghini disguised the turbo lag — a common complaint leveled at rivals making the same switch.

At a starting price of $347,000, the Temerario undercuts most of the hypercar-tier drops on this list by a wide margin, and it's the most attainable genuinely new supercar on the market this year.
