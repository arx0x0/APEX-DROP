About APEX/DROP

APEX/DROP is a demo automotive news and "drop tracker" site built for MSE543's team term project. It exists to give us a realistic system to usability-test — it is not a commercial product.

What this site does

APEX/DROP aggregates upcoming and recently announced luxury and sports car releases ("drops") into one browsable index, alongside short-form editorial coverage. The intended user is a car enthusiast who currently keeps up with releases across many separate outlets and wants a single place to track them.

What we track

This site is instrumented with Google Analytics 4 to record page views, navigation paths, and time on page, consistent with a standard production analytics setup. We also track a small number of custom events tied to specific interactions built for this project:

add_to_garage — fires when a visitor saves a car to their comparison list ("My Garage"). newsletter_signup — fires when a visitor submits the Drop Alerts email form. filter_drops and sort_drops — fire when a visitor filters or re-sorts the Drops index.

No real email addresses are stored or sent anywhere — the newsletter form is a research prototype. The "My Garage" feature stores your selections locally in your own browser (localStorage) and is not shared with anyone.

Disclaimer

This is a student project for a university course on user experience research. Car specifications shown are drawn from public reporting on expected/announced 2026 model-year releases and may include manufacturer claims, estimates, or figures that change before final production. Nothing on this site should be used to make a real purchasing decision.
