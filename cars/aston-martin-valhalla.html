Aston Martin Valhalla

Aston's most track-focused road car yet, with F1-derived aero and a genuinely unfinished edge.

Read the analysis →

After years of delay, the Valhalla is finally reaching customers in 2026. Early drive impressions describe a car that still feels a little unfinished around the edges — but promising enough that Aston Martin's bet on a proper mid-engined hybrid supercar looks like it's paying off.

The drivetrain combines a twin-turbo V8 with two front motors and a third on the crank, for a combined 1,064 hp. Aston is pairing that with an aerodynamics package developed alongside its Formula 1 program, aimed squarely at Ferrari's incoming 849 Testarossa and McLaren's next hypercar.

What's notable in early reports is how the car handles at the limit: reviewers note it's noticeably livelier and less locked-down than its aero numbers might suggest — more old-school than the wind-tunnel spec sheet implies.

At roughly $800K, the Valhalla sits in the thick of a genuinely stacked 2026 hypercar field. Deliveries are underway now, which puts Aston ahead of several of its direct rivals in actually getting cars to owners.
