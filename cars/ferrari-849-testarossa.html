Ferrari 849 Testarossa

Ferrari revives the Testarossa name for the SF90's hybrid successor.

Read the analysis →

Ferrari's replacement for the SF90 is carrying one of the most loaded names in the brand's history: Testarossa. Reviving it for a hybrid, all-wheel-drive flagship is a deliberately provocative choice, and it's already split opinion among longtime Ferrari watchers.

Mechanically, expect continuity with the SF90's formula rather than a reinvention: a twin-turbo V8 paired with a tri-motor hybrid system, estimated around 1,035 hp, with all-wheel drive handling the torque delivery. Early estimates put 0–62 mph at roughly 2.3 seconds.

Ferrari hasn't confirmed final specs or pricing — most of what's circulating is informed estimate rather than factory figure — but a full reveal is expected in the second half of 2026, ahead of the car that may matter even more to Ferrari's future: its first fully electric model.

For now, the 849 Testarossa is shaping up as the more conventional half of Ferrari's 2026 story. The electric car carrying the brand into genuinely new territory is still to come.
