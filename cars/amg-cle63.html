Mercedes-AMG CLE 63

A grand tourer built for drivers who want supercar-adjacent pace without giving up back seats.

Read the analysis →

The CLE 63 is AMG's case that a two-door grand tourer can still deliver genuinely supercar-adjacent numbers without sacrificing the comfort and usability that separates a GT car from a track toy.

It's built around a twin-turbo V8 — AMG staying with a traditional high-performance powertrain layout while most of its rivals shift toward heavier hybrid systems, producing more power and torque than several of AMG's previous naturally aspirated V8s while improving efficiency.

Inside, AMG is pitching the usual blend of German craftsmanship and driver-focused tech, aimed at buyers cross-shopping the Porsche 911 and BMW's next M-badged coupes rather than outright hypercars.

Orders are open now, positioning the CLE 63 as one of the more attainable performance drops of the year — and a useful benchmark for how much runway is left in the traditional combustion GT-car formula.
